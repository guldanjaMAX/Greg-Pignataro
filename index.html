Origin

The same thing keeps happening.

Something breaks. And something truer shows up underneath. This has happened again and again in Greg's life. With soccer. With purpose. With childhood pain. With marriage. With his own body. With himself.

He is not a coach or a therapist in the normal sense. He is someone who shows people that their body was never the enemy. And he proves it in a single afternoon.

Every big moment in Greg's life follows the same shape: a false belief gets shattered, something more real takes its place, and what felt like the worst thing turns out to be the most important door he ever walked through.

College
Earned a psychology degree while playing Division I soccer at Marquette University. The study of the mind and the demands of the body were never separate paths. They were always the same path.
Age 18
Torn ACL during a routine pass. Everything he thought he was got ripped away in one moment. He traded the athlete identity for a bigger question: who am I when I cannot perform?
Age 29
Three words at a bachelor party changed everything. A weight he had carried his whole life lifted. For the first time, he felt peace, connection, and love without conditions. The month that followed rewired his entire life.
Age 33
Decades of held emotion released in a 15 minute cry. His hip mobility changed overnight. Days later, he set the Guinness World Record for Nordic Hamstring Curls. 24 reps in 60 seconds. The body does not lie.
Training
Certified under Dr. Gabor Maté, one of the world's leading experts on trauma and the body. This training gave a clinical framework to what Greg had already been living: the body holds what the mind will not face.
Now
Cofounder of Enlightened Health. World record holder. Creator of a new kind of work that dissolves the line between physical training and deep personal transformation. Based in Phoenix, Arizona.

The Shadow

The parts you hide.

This is where the real work lives. Underneath the goals, the achievements, the curated version of yourself, there are parts that got pushed away a long time ago. Anger. Grief. Shame. Fear. The body held them when the mind could not.

Shadow work is not about fixing what is wrong. It is about welcoming what has been exiled. When you stop running from these parts, something remarkable happens: the tension that lived in your body for decades can finally let go.

This is not comfortable work. But it is the most important work. And Greg, trained by Dr. Gabor Maté in the connection between trauma and the body, holds space for it with both clinical precision and deep compassion.
